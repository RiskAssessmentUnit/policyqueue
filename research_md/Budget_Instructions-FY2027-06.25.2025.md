## Evidence (quotes)

> For this upcoming budget cycle, agencies should continue to evaluate, improve, and refine performance measures based on last summer’s review by agencies, the Division of the Budget and the Kansas Legislative Research Department and based on the review of performance measures by the Legislature this past session.

> Please see the Division of the Budget’s website (https://budget.kansas.gov/pbb-resources/) for resources on PBB.

> Agencies should submit budgets using the regular budget process and the strategic planning process discussed below.

> Only in this way can rational decisions be made within limited budgetary resources at the different levels, with a clear understanding of the effect of those inputs on outputs and outcomes.

> One of the biggest environmental factors for state agencies is the state’s fiscal status.  No discussion of an organization’s operating environment would be complete without an understanding of the revenue and expenditure estimates of the state as a whole, as well as of individual agencies.

> As required by KSA 75-3717, the narrative description for each new or expanded program must include an estimate of anticipated expenditures that would be required to support the new or expanded program for the budget year and three ensuing fiscal years

> The Kansas Information Technology Office (KITO) supports the statutory responsibilities of the executive branch Chief Information Technology Officer (CITO) and the Chief Information Technology Architect (CITA).

> Longevity bonuses will be budgeted at $40 per year of service for employees hired prior to June 15, 2008 (KSA 75-5541).
